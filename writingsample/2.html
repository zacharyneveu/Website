Uncompressed audio encoding is how music is stored on CD’s. This method of storing
audio is the simplest of the three techniques, basically storing the sound simply as a list of the
readings of each sample. Within uncompressed audio encoding, there are several speciﬁc formats.
Pulse Code Modulation or PCM is the format used to store sound on CD’s. For computers, two
different competing formats (codecs) have long lead the market for uncompressed audio.
Microsoft’s WAV format is the standard for windows computers while Apple’s AIFF format is a
direct competitor to it. Uncompressed audio has an identical bitrate to the original studio
recording. This means that the quality is high, but ﬁle sizes are very large creating the need for
compressed formats.
