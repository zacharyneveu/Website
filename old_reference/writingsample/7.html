Based on this comparison, what can be said about the possibility of the widespread
adoption of some codecs over others? It can be seen fairly quickly that certain codecs have
shortcomings that rule out their widespread adoption. Monkey’s Audio is overly difﬁcult for a
computer to decode, making it a poor choice for mobile players and an unlikely choice for a
popular lossless codec. Likewise, both Apple formats, ALAC and AAC are unlikely to become a
cross platform standard because they do not lead the market in any particular respect and are only
supported by one brand. Among remaining lossy codecs, MP3 will likely remain the most
popular for some time as it is the current universal standard, but is likely to eventually be replaced
by the increasingly popular, better quality per bit Ogg Vorbis format. Among lossless codecs,
FLAC has emerged as a pretty clear leader. It is fast and easy both to encode and decode, it has
considerable compatibility, and the audio quality is lossless.
Conclusions
Out of the two remaining formats, Ogg Vorbis appears to be better than or the same as
FLAC in every category except quality: it has much smaller ﬁle sizes, it decodes and encodes
